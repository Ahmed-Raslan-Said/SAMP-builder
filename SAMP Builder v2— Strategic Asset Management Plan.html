📦 Module 2

Asset Inventory

Document what you own, what it's worth, and calculate the Lifecycle Expenditure Indicator for each asset class.

📊Asset Classes & Financial Values

Enter values in AED. The Lifecycle Expenditure Indicator is calculated automatically.

Asset Class Gross Replacement Cost
Gross Replacement Cost (GRC)
What it would cost to replace this asset TODAY with a new equivalent — regardless of age or condition.
Use current market rates for materials and labor
GRC = Unit Rate × Quantity
 Carrying Value
Carrying Value (Book Value)
Current worth after accounting for years of use. GRC minus accumulated depreciation.
Should decrease each year unless revalued
CV = GRC − Accumulated Depreciation
 Annual Depreciation
Annual Depreciation
How much value the asset loses each year. Used to estimate future renewal needs.
Typically: GRC ÷ Design Life
Dep = GRC ÷ Design Life (straight-line)
 Actual Annual Spend
Actual Annual Spend
What you actually spend on this asset per year: operation + maintenance + renewal combined.
Compare to Lifecycle Cost to get the Indicator
 Lifecycle Cost (Should Spend)
Lifecycle Cost (Should Spend)
What you SHOULD spend annually for sustainable management: operation + maintenance + depreciation.
If unsure, enter: Op + Maint + Depreciation
LC = Operation + Maintenance + Depreciation
 Indicator
Lifecycle Expenditure Indicator
Ratio of actual spending to required spending. The key health metric for each asset class.
90-110% = healthy | <60% = underfunded | >130% = heavy renewal
Indicator = Actual Spend ÷ Lifecycle Cost × 100

PoF — Probability of Failure
Probability of Failure (PoF)
How likely is this asset to fail during the assessment period?
1=Rare | 2=Unlikely | 3=Possible | 4=Likely | 5=Almost Certain
CoF — Consequence of Failure
Consequence of Failure (CoF)
How bad would it be if this asset failed? Consider safety, finance, environment, service.
1=Negligible | 2=Minor | 3=Moderate | 4=Severe | 5=Catastrophic
Risk Score = PoF × CoF
